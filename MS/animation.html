Pro-democracy Protest

Pro-democracy protesters are gathering at Causeway Bay to protest against Beijing's move to initiate the process of implementing national security law in Hong Kong on May 22. The move comes after the National People's Congress put forward a resolution on May 22 to introduce a law aimed at stopping any act that threatens national security. According to article 23 of the Basic Law, the city’s mini-constitution, Hong Kong must enact national security laws to prohibit treason, secession, sedition and subversion against the central government. The legislation failed in 2003 following mass protests.

  • Date: 24 May 2020

Korean Flourist

Although floral arrangement has a long history, it became popular in Hong Kong only in recent years. The arrangements are often considered to be luxury items used during celebrations or anniversaries, rather than an artform. In 2017, famous Korean florist Vanessa Lee Ju-yeon introduced Korean floral design classes in Hong Kong, which popularised this form of art. Since then, Korean florists have visited Hong Kong to teach floral design. Mr. Jang is one of them and he is teaching here for the second time
